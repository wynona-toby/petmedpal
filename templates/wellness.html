Beagles are not a high maintenance dog. Beagles are an intelligent and affectionate breed of dog that require minimal maintenance and are relatively low-maintenance compared to other breeds. Beagles love attention and affection, but they need a lot of time outside. They have a superior sense of smell, and love to use their working instincts to 'hunt' scents outdoors.

 LIVING TEMPERATURE:
 Beagles, as a breed, are quite adaptable to different weather conditions. However, they are most comfortable in temperatures ranging from 60°F to 75°F (15°C to 24°C). In general, temperatures above 85°F (29°C) can be considered too hot for beagles and may put them at risk of overheating.

 DAILY CARE:
 As long as your groomer is using a safe shampoo, a beagle can be given a bath as often as once per day, but normally every 2,4,6 or 8 weeks. Whilst a Beagle's coat is easy to clean and care for, they should still be groomed at least once a week. Grooming can be a good time to check your dog for any lumps or signs of parasites, as well as their ears for signs of infection such as brown or black wax and a strange smell.

 FOOD TIMINGS AND DIET:
 As a general rule, adult Beagles will require about 1.5 to 2 cups of high-quality dog food per day, divided into two meals. Beagles are known to have a strong appetite. Meat, bone, offal and a small amount of plant
ingredients are all important ingredients in the diet of a Beagle. A raw diet contains, on average, 70% moisture/ water, which helps to keep your Beagle properly hydrated.

GOLDEN RETRIEVER

Golden Retrievers are very active dogs and need plenty of opportunities to exercise, play and get rid of excess energy. Your Golden Retriever will need a minimum of two hours of good exercise per day.In some ways, like grooming, socializing with other dogs, and exercising, Golden Retrievers can be considered high maintenance. However, these pooches are also super adaptable, friendly with other people and dogs, and can be easily trained.

 LIVING TEMPERATURE: 
 Golden Retrievers thrive in moderate temperatures similar to what humans find comfortable. A room temperature range of 68-75°F (20-24°C) is
generally considered ideal.

 DAILY CARE:
 You can bathe golden retrievers every 4-6 weeks or as often as necessary. Bathing them too often could inflict major harm on their double coat. Instead, focus on regular brushing and grooming to remove loose hair, dirt, and debris.
Make it a habit to spend some quality time brushing your dog to keep the coat separated and divided which, in turn, will allow the skin to breathe.

 FOOD TIMINGS AND DIET:
 DO NOT free feed your golden; instead feed a measured amount of food twice a day. Most adult goldens will eat 1 cup of premium dog food twice a day. Meat, bone, offal and a small amount of plant ingredients are all important ingredients in the diet of a Golden Retriever, each adding highly beneficial vitamins and minerals to meal times.









Siamese cats are not well suited to the great outdoors and should not be allowed to roam outside unsupervised. They are not considered high maintenance in terms of grooming but they are high maintenance when it comes to playtime, cuddle time and general interaction with you.

DAILY CARE:
 A bath every 4-6 weeks is usually sufficient, unless they get into something particularly messy. With their short coat laying flat against their skin,
Siamese cats do not require any particular grooming. Weekly combing will be more than enough to remove dead hairs and distribute skin oils. 

 FOOD TIMINGS AND DIET:
 A Siamese kitten should be fed between 3 - 4 meals per day. A combination of wet and dry food crafted with high quality and natural ingredients will work well for your Siamese cat.


LIVING TEMPERATURE :
 Siamese cats prefer temperatures between 75-86°F.

DOMESTIC SHORTHAIR


 Although Domestic Shorthair cats are great first-time cats, owners should have time to play with them because of their playful nature. If possible, Domestic Shorthair cats should have plenty of time outdoors. If you have a big back garden, or you live in a rural area, you're the ideal Domestic Shorthair cat owner.

 LIVING TEMPERATURE:
 Your cat needs to maintain a body temperature of above 90 degrees to ward off hypothermia, so keeping your thermostat around 70 degrees
is a good way to ensure they'll stay cozy all winter long.

 DAILY CARE:
In general, cats should be given a bath once every 4-6 weeks, depending on how often they groom themselves, and the environment they're usually in. Domestic short-haired cats generally do a good job of grooming themselves. However, grooming sessions at least once a week are still important to help keep their coat in a good condition.

 FOOD TIMINGS AND DIET:
 Cats should eat at least two meals each day, about 12 hours apart. But a breakfast, lunch, afternoon, dinner, and right before bed schedule is an equally great option. If more than 12 hours elapses between meals, the stomach can become hyperacidic causing nausea. Cat should be provided high protein cat food with high-quality animal meat as well as gut-friendly probiotics or prebiotics to keep our moggies' tummies healthy and poops happy.
